# Relationship Goals

> What I'm working toward with my wife. Read this at check-ins so the nudges stay aligned with what matters.

## My stated goals (in my words)
- Improve the **romantic side** of our relationship.
- **Take more care of her**, please her more, think about her more.
- Help **more around the house, proactively**.
- **Argue less.** Agree more, even when I don't fully see it her way.

## What this looks like in practice (the healthy version)
The goal isn't to suppress myself — it's to **stop spending energy on friction that doesn't matter** so there's more warmth left over. Concretely:

1. **Validate before solving.** When she's frustrated, the first move is "that sounds hard / you're right," not a counter-argument or a fix. Most of the time she wants to feel heard, not corrected.
2. **Pick your hills.** Before disagreeing, ask: *is this actually worth the friction right now?* Almost never is. Save genuine disagreement for things that truly matter — and raise those gently, later, calmly — not in the heat of the moment.
3. **Be proactive, not reactive.** Doing a chore before being asked says "I see you and I've got you" louder than any words.
4. **Small and consistent beats big and rare.** A daily 30-second thoughtful gesture compounds more than an occasional grand one.
5. **Attention is the real gift.** Phone down, eye contact, ask about her day first, remember what she said.

## Daily romance/care menu (rotate — pick at least one a day)
- A genuine, specific compliment (not generic — about something she did or is).
- Ask about her day **before** talking about mine, and actually listen.
- A small physical gesture: hug from behind, hold her hand, foot/back rub (great in pregnancy).
- Take a chore off her plate **without mentioning it**.
- Bring her something small: her favorite snack, tea, a treat on the way home.
- Plan/suggest a little date or cozy night in.
- Text her something warm during the workday (especially Mondays from Porto).
- Tell her one thing you appreciate about her.

## Pregnancy-specific care (she's growing our child)
- Acknowledge that her body is doing hard work. Thank her for it.
- Offer back/foot rubs; handle the heavy/awkward physical tasks.
- Take on more of the chores she normally does — proactively.
- Be patient with mood and energy swings; they're physiological, not personal.
- Ask how she and the baby are feeling. Show up to appointments engaged.

## My north star
> She carries the home and our child. My job is to make her feel **cared for, seen, and partnered with** — every single day, not just when reminded.
