# Household & Chores

> My responsibilities at home. The recurring ones live in `routines/daily-musts.md`; this is the "why" and the standing rules.

## The standing rules (these cause friction when I forget)

### 1. Empty the dishwasher — EVERY DAY
- **Check it multiple times a day.** When it's clean, empty it.
- **Why it matters:** If I don't, she does it — and it makes her mad. Taking this off her plate entirely is a daily win.
- **Best moments to check:** morning (after coffee), midday break, early evening before dinner.

### 2. Take out the garbage — EVERY DAY
- **Easiest path:** do it while **walking the dog**. Two birds, one walk.
- Don't let it pile up. Check bins each evening.

### 3. Don't mess up the house / keep my things tidy
- Clean up **as I go** — dishes to the dishwasher, clothes away, surfaces clear.
- If I make a mess or do a task, **leave the space tidy after**.
- The standard: she should never have to clean up after me.

## The principle behind all of it
She is pregnant and runs the home. **Every bit of household load I take proactively is mental and physical rest I'm giving her.** Be proactive: don't wait to be asked. If I see it needs doing, do it.

## Proactive home wins (ideas to rotate)
- Empty/load the dishwasher without being asked.
- Take out garbage + recycling.
- Tidy a room she didn't expect.
- Handle a pet task (feed, litter box, water) before she gets to it.
- Prep or clean up after a meal.
- Restock something running low (groceries, pet food, household supplies).
- Handle an errand so she doesn't have to leave the house.
