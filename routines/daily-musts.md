# Daily Musts — the non-negotiables

> These reset every day. Each check-in shows the full checklist with simple, concrete steps. No vague items — every line is something I can do in minutes.

> **Key rule:** she is specific about how things are done. Learn her way. Do it right. Don't make her redo it. And NEVER wait to be asked — being proactive is the whole point.

## Kitchen
- [ ] Empty the dishwasher (check morning / midday / pre-dinner)
- [ ] Load dirty dishes into dishwasher — don't leave them in the sink
- [ ] Wipe kitchen counters and stovetop after every meal
- [ ] Take out the garbage (do it on the dog walk)
- [ ] Check all bins in the house, not just the kitchen

## Laundry
- [ ] Check the washing machine — if clothes are done, hang or fold them NOW
- [ ] Put away any dry/folded clothes — don't let them pile up

## Living spaces
- [ ] Make the bed (if she hasn't already)
- [ ] Scan living room — put away anything out of place
- [ ] Scan bedroom — put away my things, clear surfaces
- [ ] Sweep or vacuum kitchen and living room if needed

## Pets
- [ ] Dog walked (~11:00)
- [ ] Check pet bowls — food and water topped up
- [ ] Litter box cleaned (she CANNOT do this — pregnancy risk)

## Maintenance & restock
- [ ] Quick scan: anything broken, leaking, running low? Fix or buy it before she notices
- [ ] Restock consumables if low (toilet paper, soap, pet food, her snacks)

## Her
- [ ] Ask about her day and actually listen
- [ ] One caring act (see `context/relationship-goals.md` → daily menu)

## Monday extras (Porto days)
- [ ] Send a warm message during the day
- [ ] Bring something home for her (treat, flowers, small gift)

## Why this matters
She is 24+ weeks pregnant and carries the home. Every chore I do proactively — before she asks — is mental and physical rest I'm giving her. The goal: she never has to ask, remind, or redo.
